# Brand Strategy — Discovery Questions & Coaching

Ask one at a time. After each answer, run it against the coaching triggers below; deliver the nudge if one fires, then let them revise or move on. Capture raw answers; polish in synthesis.

## Coaching Triggers

| Trigger | How to detect | Response |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Too short / shallow | A few words, no texture | A specific follow-up that forces detail |
| Marketing speak | "save time," "empowered," "seamless," "game-changer," "world-class," "cutting-edge," "innovative solution," "next-level," "best-in-class" | "What does that actually look or feel like?" |
| Too generic | Could describe any brand in any category | "What makes that specifically *this* brand?" |
| Muse with no visual | An inspiration named for its mission, not its look | "What can we *steal* visually? If nothing, it's not a muse for us." |
| Tactical when emotional is needed | Features/logistics when asked about feeling | "Set the logistics aside — how should it *feel*?" |
| Asks you for the answers | "Just suggest some muses / pick a direction for us" | Offer the deeper path first ("your own muses will fit better — want to try a few questions first?"); generate ideas only after they decline, framed as starting points to react to |

## 1. Vision & Truth
- What is this brand really for — beyond what it sells?
- What do you believe about this space that your competitors don't?
- If the product disappeared tomorrow, what would the world miss?

## 2. The Ideal User
- Describe one real person this is for. Not a segment — a person.
- What do they already think is beautiful? Trustworthy? Premium? Fun?
- Where do they spend attention, and what does *that* look like?

## 3. Competitive Visual Landscape
- Who are the 3–5 brands you're visually compared to?
- What colors, type, and imagery does the category default to?
- What would feel *wrong* or off-category here — and is that an opportunity?

## 4. Brand Personality
- If the brand walked into a room, who is it? Be specific.
- Pick three adjectives — now make each one specific. "Confident" *like whom*?
- What is the brand definitely *not*?

## 5. The Muses (spend the most time here — and SHOW them)
A muse is **not** a favorite or a thing to feature — it's a thing that makes *you* feel the target feeling. Always ask anchored to it: *"You want people to feel [X]. What makes *you* feel [X] — specifically, visually?"* If they answer with something they "love" or "want to show," check it: does it produce the feeling? If not, it's not a muse.
For every muse named: fetch real images and put them on screen before accepting it. Confirm against the image ("which part of this is the feeling?"), add it to the muse board, and record the image source in the working doc. A muse without a confirmed image isn't captured yet.
- *"What makes you feel [the target feeling]?"* — a film, a product, a place, an object, an interface, a material, another brand. (Not what you love; what produces the feeling.)
- For each: **what exactly would we steal?** A color story? A texture? A type treatment? A sense of space? A quality of light?
- Push past the obvious in-category muses. The best DNA usually comes from outside the category.

## 6. Emotional Target (Big Three)
- Of everything we've surfaced, what are the three feelings this brand must produce?
- For each: how would we *know* a design produced it?

## 7. Fixed Core vs. Flexible Range
- What must *always* be present for this to be recognizably the brand?
- Where is the brand free to stretch — campaigns, premium tiers, seasonal, sub-brands?
- Imagine a piece that drops the everyday font and color entirely. What would still have to be true for it to count as on-brand?
